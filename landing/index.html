The problem

You need the log.
You cannot risk the key.

Production incidents still mean sharing stack traces, env snippets, and support bundles with ChatGPT, Claude, vendors, or a teammate. Manual find-and-replace is slow. Over-stripping kills the story.

The 2 a.m. paste problem

Something is on fire. You open an AI chat or a vendor ticket. The log that would unblock you also holds API keys, emails, tokens, and customer IDs.

  1. Share raw and hope
  2. Scrub by hand for 20 minutes
  3. Send a useless redacted blob

Enterprise scanners solve a different job

Secret scanners find leaks in git and CI. They do not hand you a reviewed, ready-to-share copy of this file right now.

Trust is the product

If the safe path is slower than the risky path, people take the risk. SecretScrub makes the safe path the fast one.

Workflow

Sensitive log → shareable copy in three moves

One primary workflow. No security dashboard. No cloud upload for the scan itself.

  1. 01

    Drop or pipe

    Bring the artifact

    File, folder, paste, or stdin. Logs, JSON, YAML, env, and plain text. Originals stay immutable.

  2. 02

    Scan locally

    Review what changed

    Common secrets become semantic placeholders like [STRIPE_SECRET#1]. Same value, same placeholder inside one scrub.

  3. 03

    Export safe copy

    Share with confidence

    Get a usable artifact plus a summary of what was redacted. Unsupported files are marked for review — never silently labeled safe.

FAQ

Straight answers

Trust is earned with limits said out loud, not buried in fine print.

Does SecretScrub upload my logs?

No. Scan, review, and export run on your device in the default product. There is no hosted artifact storage tier in the initial release. Optional license or update checks, when shipped, will be documented and disable-able.

Will it catch every secret?

No tool can. SecretScrub targets common provider tokens, JWTs, emails, IPs, and your custom rules. It never claims universal safety. Unsupported or partial cases stay in “Review required.”

How is this different from TruffleHog or GitGuardian?

Those products find and manage secret sprawl in repos and pipelines. SecretScrub prepares a single artifact so you can share it safely: redaction, consistent placeholders, structure preservation, and export.

Can I use it offline?

Yes for the core scrub workflow. The free CLI works offline. Pro is designed for offline use after license activation.

What formats are supported?

Private beta focuses on plain text, logs, JSON, YAML, and env files. TOML and binary formats are marked unsupported rather than fake-safe. More formats only after demand and security review.

Is the free CLI really free?

Yes. It is the try-before-buy path: standard detectors, local path/stdin, structured findings. Limits may apply on bundle size so Pro remains the full desktop workflow.
